Understanding how your score is calculated will help you increase your score without making needless mistakes.

Everybody knows you have to make your payments on-time, but it is not the only factor in a great credit score.

What Makes Up Your Credit Score?

How it's Broken Down

35% Payment History
30% Amount Owed
15% Length of History
10% New Credit
10% Credit Type Mix

Things you SHOULD do...
Try to pay down your credit card balances - the ideal percentage of use is under 10%, so if you have a card with a $1000 limit - try to get the balance down to around $100 if possible.
Continue to make all your credit payments on-time. Setting up an automated bill pay with you bank can save you if you forget to make a payment.
Keep a healthy mix of credit types - Credit Cards, Personal Loans, Mortgage, Car Loans etc.
Never close old accounts if possible, it counts towards your credit history.
Use your credit cards - if there is no activity, there is nothing positive to report to the bureaus.
If you are unable to pay down all your cards to under 10% balance, try to at least maintain a 30% balance across all cards. Anything above 30% will be doing you no favors.
